Description

This ontology describes classes of geological and administrative feature relevant to the duties of the Geological Survey of Queensland.

As subclasses of the GeoSPARQL Ontology's Feature class, this ontology defines Geological Feature and Administrative Feature. Multiple subclasses of those two main classes are also defined.

Most of the subclasses of Geological Feature defined here are not disjoint with other subclasses of it in recognition of the fact that a real-world Feature may be classed in multiple ways. Administrative Feature subclasses, however, are all disjoint having been defined disjointly by people.

References to and positioning of other Features defined elsewhere are made, such as placing the HY_Features model's Catchment and CatchmentAggregate classes as subclasses of this ontology's Basin class (a subclass of Geological Feature). As a result, the classes of object within the LocI project's Geofabric dataset can be related to objects classified using this ontology.

GroundwaterBasinc

IRI http://linked.data.gov.au/def/geoadminfeatures#GroundwaterBasin
Description A Groundwater Basin is a subsurface, three-dimensional, water-storage system contained by porous sediments and/or sedimentary rocks comprising an aquifer or a succession of stacked aquifers that are separated by non-porous and generally impermeable sediments or rocks (termed acquitards or, if completely impermeable, aquicludes). Groundwater Basins have recharge and discharge zones, the latter where water flows back to the surface at springs or into rivers, lakes, etc.
Super-classes geoaf:Basin c

SedimentaryBasinc

IRI http://linked.data.gov.au/def/geoadminfeatures#SedimentaryBasin
Description A Sedimentary Basin comprises a three-dimensional region in which sediments accumulate or have accumulated from a range of varying geological/tectonic causes. Present-day sedimentary basins include the internal-draining Lake Eyre Basin (central Australia), but, from a geological perspective, primary focus is given to resource-rich, Sedimentary Basins that formed, and were subsequently terminated by a causative event (e.g., tectonic uplift), at varying times in the geological past.
